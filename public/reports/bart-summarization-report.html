Project Report

BART Summarization Pipeline

This project explores whether continued self-supervised adaptation improves the summarization performance of a pretrained BART model on XSum news data.

Project Overview

The notebook compares two starting points for abstractive summarization. One model begins from the original pretrained BART checkpoint, while the other begins from a checkpoint that was first adapted through masked denoising on article text. The aim was to determine whether the extra adaptation step leads to better domain fit before supervised fine-tuning.

How It Was Created

The workflow was organized in three stages. First, the notebook loads the XSum dataset and the facebook/bart-base checkpoint. Second, it continues self-supervised denoising training on article text only. Third, it fine-tunes two models on summarization under the same supervised setup and compares them using ROUGE scores and generated example summaries.
