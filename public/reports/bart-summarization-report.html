BART Summarization Pipeline

This article covers an encoder-decoder NLP workflow built around XSum and facebook/bart-base, with an explicit comparison between original pretrained weights and a domain-adapted checkpoint.

Transformers BART XSum ROUGE

What the project does

The notebook asks whether a lightweight domain-adaptation step helps BART summarize BBC-style news text more effectively. The goal is not just to fine-tune a model, but to measure whether the extra self-supervised stage adds practical value.

How it was built

The implementation follows three steps: load the XSum subset and checkpoint, continue masked denoising on article text only, then fine-tune two models and compare them using ROUGE and generated examples. That staged setup gives the final comparison a clear experimental basis.

Why the image is here

The article visual is a compact rendering of the real model path used in the notebook. It is included so the article can explain the workflow quickly without forcing the reader to decode notebook cells first.
